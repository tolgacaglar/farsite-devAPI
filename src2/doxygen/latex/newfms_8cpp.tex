The FMS function library models fuel stick temperature and moisture content given an input weather stream of air temperature, air humidity, solar radiation, and cumulative rainfall.

The fms.\+c and fms.\+h files contain functions to (1) create fuel moisture sticks of various sizes, (2) initialize the stick\textquotesingle{}s internal and external environment and clock, (3) update the stick\textquotesingle{}s external environment at arbitrary time intervals, thereby forcing its internal environment to be updated according to Ralph Nelson\textquotesingle{}s stick moisture model, (4) determine the volume-\/weighted mean moisture content of the stick, and (5) destroy the stick and its memory resources.

In addition to the above "{}public"{} functions, there are a number of "{}private"{} functions to configure and reinitialize the stick and to calculate and convert time and date information.\hypertarget{newfms_8cpp_usage}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{Usage}{Usage}}\label{newfms_8cpp_usage}
The demo.\+c program shows how to use the public FMS functions. Five or six functions are normally used\+:

1 Call one or more of the stick creation functions ( \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_ad4fca6eaa9f1a2fda723707084c5a5e3}{Fms\+\_\+\+Create1\+Hour()}, \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_afeee5de13f926fe5fa8afe2b3d9cf866}{Fms\+\_\+\+Create10\+Hour()}, \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_ae2af8bbeb969dfa276fb3f70f38f81bb}{Fms\+\_\+\+Create100\+Hour()}, or \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_aad47023ed1e75df91c0635346be8ddfc}{Fms\+\_\+\+Create1000\+Hour()}) to create a fuel moisture stick object.

2 Call \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_a46803007969d17a0567ac1ec40f63a94}{Fms\+\_\+\+Initialize\+At()} to initialize the stick\textquotesingle{}s environment and internal state at a given date and time.

3 Call \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_a934c37af225a142048d27c36de63ae72}{Fms\+\_\+\+Update\+At()} whenever a new weather observation is available, passing it the new date, time, air temperature, air humidity, solar radiation, and cumulative rainfall parameters. The stick\textquotesingle{}s moisture profile is automatically updated for the current conditions.

4 Call \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_af9d70e57ae6d7bee37f8261a91b73f27}{Fms\+\_\+\+Mean\+Wtd\+Moisture()} whenever you need the stick\textquotesingle{}s volume-\/ weighted mean moisture content.

5 Call \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_a6288a67f07a4a6033c23db09d57caec2}{Fms\+\_\+\+Destroy()} once for each stick you created.

6 See \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_a7233c96335ab751c7129b22d50462e05}{Fms\+\_\+\+Write\+To\+Ascii\+Stream()} and \doxylink{newfms_8cpp_a6243504569b6b6e169cf2387a0274b6e}{Fms\+\_\+\+Read\+From\+Ascii\+Stream()} for examples of how to store and load sticks to/from files.\hypertarget{newfms_8cpp_manifest}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{File List}{File List}}\label{newfms_8cpp_manifest}

{\footnotesize\ttfamily double CDT\+\_\+\+Julian\+Date (\begin{DoxyParamCaption}\item[{int}]{year,  }\item[{int}]{month,  }\item[{int}]{day,  }\item[{int}]{hour,  }\item[{int}]{minute,  }\item[{int}]{second,  }\item[{int}]{millisecond }\end{DoxyParamCaption})}



Calculates the Julian date from the passed date and time. 

The Julian Date is the number of days that have elapsed since {\itshape noon}, 12h Universal Time on January 1, 4713 B.\+C.

The Julian Date was developed in 1583 by French scholar Joseph Justus Scaliger. The beginning day of January 1, 4713 B.\+C. is the previous coincidence of the 28-\/year solar cycle, the 19-\/year Golden Number (Metonic) lunar cycle, and the 15-\/year indiction cycle used for Roman taxation.

The Gregorian calendar reform is taken into account when calculating the Julian Date. Thus the day following 1582 October 4 is 1582 October 15. ~\newline
 The Julian calendar is used through 1582 Oct 4 (leap years every 4th year), ~\newline
 and the Gregorian calendar is used for dates on and after 1582 Oct 15 (leap year exceptions). Together, the Julian-\/\+Gregorian calendar system may be referred to simply as the Western calendar.

The "{}\+B.\+C."{} years are counted astronomically; the year before A.\+D. 1 is year 0, and the year preceeding this is -\/1.
